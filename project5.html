Neethling Virus Detection in Cows and Buffalos

Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD), caused by the Neethling virus, is a highly contagious and economically devastating disease affecting cattle and buffaloes worldwide. In this project, I developed a deep learning-based image classification system to detect LSD infections from cattle and buffalo images using a curated and preprocessed dataset. By building, evaluating, and ensembling five state-of-the-art models, and deploying the final solution as a web application, I aimed to enable rapid, accessible, and reliable disease screening to help prevent outbreaks and minimize livestock losses.

Data Acquisition and Preprocessing:
• Collected 1,960 images from two Kaggle datasets, combining healthy and infected (lumpy skin) cattle and buffalo images.
• Manually filtered and cleaned the dataset to remove 414 mislabeled, low-quality, or irrelevant images, resulting in a final dataset of 1,546 images (1,058 healthy, 488 infected).
• Standardized all images to 256x256 pixels, normalized pixel values, and split the data into training (70%), validation (20%), and test (10%) sets.

Model Development:
• Used Python with TensorFlow, OpenCV, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn for model development, training, and analysis.
• Built and trained five deep learning models: a custom CNN, and four transfer learning models-VGG-16, Inception-V3, EfficientNet, and ResNet-50.
• Selected the top three models (VGG-16, Inception-V3, EfficientNet) for ensemble techniques (Max Voting, Averaging, Weighted Averaging) to further boost accuracy.

Web Application Deployment:
• Developed a secure, user-friendly web application using Flask, HTML, CSS (Bootstrap), and MongoDB for user management.
• Integrated the final ensemble model, enabling users to upload cattle images and receive instant predictions (healthy or infected).

Results:
• Individual model test accuracies ranged from 85.94% to 96.10%; ensemble techniques improved accuracy to 96.87% (Averaging method).
• Averaging (top ensemble method) achieved 96.87% accuracy and 95.4% F1-score, outperforming individual models like Inception-V3 (95.1%) and VGG-16 ResNet-50 (93.2%).
• The final solution provides rapid, accessible, and accurate disease detection, supporting early intervention and reducing the risk of widespread outbreaks.

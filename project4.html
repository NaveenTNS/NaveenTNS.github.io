Fish Ulcer Detection (Deep Learning)

Fish ulcer, or Epizootic Ulcerative Syndrome (EUS), is a highly contagious disease that poses a major threat to freshwater fish populations and the livelihoods of fish farmers worldwide. In this project, I developed a deep learning-based image classification system to detect ulcer-infected fish using a curated and augmented dataset. By building and evaluating five models, I aimed to provide a fast, accurate, and scalable solution for early disease detection, helping to prevent outbreaks and minimize economic losses in aquaculture.

Data Acquisition and Preprocessing:
• Gathered 1,216 fish images (ulcer-infected and healthy) from Google Images, then manually filtered and cleaned the dataset to remove irrelevant, redundant, and low-quality images.
• After preprocessing and augmentation (rotations, flips, shifts, zoom), the dataset was expanded and finalized at 1,036 images: 589 healthy and 447 ulcer-infected, all resized to 256x256 pixels and normalized for computational efficiency.
• The data was split into training (70%), validation (20%), and test (10%) sets.

Model Development:
• Used Python with libraries like TensorFlow, OpenCV, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn for model development, training, and result visualization.
• Built and trained five deep learning models for binary image classification: a custom CNN, and four transfer learning models-VGG-16, Inception-V3, EfficientNet, and Xception.

Result:
• VGG-16 achieved the highest test accuracy (best among all models), with perfect classification of healthy fish and only 6.4% misclassification in the ulcer class.
• All models were evaluated with confusion matrices, ROC curves, and precision-recall curves, confirming VGG-16’s superior performance.
